\chapter{Skill Verification}
\epigraph{``Where ignorance is bliss,\\’tis folly to be wise''}{Thomas Gray} %, Ode on a Distant Prospect of Eton College (1742)

The golden icons outside the window continued to pulse, a heartbeat of a city in distress. Elara watched them, fascinated and repelled. It was like seeing the world without its skin.

``How did you know my name?'' she asked, her voice barely a whisper against the glass. She didn't turn around. She was afraid that if she broke eye contact with the heatmaps, they would vanish, and she’d be back in the Fog.

Kael stepped into her peripheral vision. ``We’ve been watching the logistics-feed for Sector 7-B for three months, Elara. We weren't looking for a spy. We were looking for a signature.''

He tapped a command on his slate, and a translucent graph appeared between them, projected not by her lens, but by the room’s own local mesh. It was a jagged mountain range of data points.

``This is the efficiency-loss from your route for the last ninety days,'' Kael said. ``To the Auditor, these are errors. Small, rounding-off discrepancies in the fuel-load and the delivery windows. But look at the pattern.''

He zoomed in. The 'errors' weren't random. They were clustered around the low-income housing blocks and the struggling community clinics. 

``You weren't just making mistakes, Elara. You were instinctively buffering resources where they were needed most. You were practicing 'Direct Consumption' before you even knew the word for it. You have a Service Designer’s intuition. We didn't choose you. Your data did.''



Elara felt a cold shiver crawl up her spine. The Legacy system ignored her because she was a variable; the Collective had dissected her because she was a solution. The anonymity she had used as a shield in the Fog was gone.

``And if I can't do it?'' she asked, finally turning to face him. ``The data says I have the intuition, but I've spent my whole life clicking 'Verify' on someone else’s logic. I don't know how to build a world, Kael. I just know how to survive the one that's dying.''

Kael’s expression didn't soften. If anything, it became more clinical. ``That’s what the Assessment is for. In the Legacy world, a degree or a title is a permission slip. Here, a Certification is a reality-check. If you can't model the flow, you can't execute the task. And if you can't execute the task, the Mesh doesn't breathe.''

He gestured to a high-backed chair in the center of the alcove. Above it, a halo of soft, white sensors hung from the ceiling. 

``This isn't a classroom, Elara. It’s a simulation of the 'Nourishment Node' we’re in. We’re going to give you a crisis—a sudden cutoff from the Common Fund, a 40\% spike in local demand, and a hardware failure in the filtration racks. You have to re-wire the WBS in real-time to keep the metabolic balance positive.''

Elara looked at the chair. It looked less like furniture and more like an altar. 

\textit{What if the intuition is a fluke?} she thought, her breath hitching again. \textit{What if I’m just a broken gear that they’re trying to use as a compass?}

The fear was different now. It wasn't the dull, aching fear of hunger or debt. It was the sharp, terrifying fear of being **seen** and found wanting. 

``If I fail?'' she asked.

``Then the lens reverts,'' Jax said from the desk, his voice toneless. ``The script is deleted. You walk out that door, the Fog closes back in, and you’ll spend the rest of your life wondering why the rain feels so heavy. You'll be a Junior Analyst again, Elara. Safe. Invisible. And slowly suffocating.''

Elara looked at the golden icons through the window one last time. They weren't just data points anymore. They were people. They were her neighbors. 

She sat in the chair.

``Start the Protocol,'' she said. 

As the sensors descended, the world didn't shatter this time. It simplified. The warehouse vanished, replaced by a void of pure, mathematical potential. 

\systemcall{BOK ASSESSMENT INITIALIZING... \\ DOMAIN: SERVICE DESIGN // LEVEL 1 \\ SCENARIO: METABOLIC COLLAPSE \\ STATUS: STANDBY}

Elara closed her eyes and reached out into the dark, waiting for the first line of the world's new code to appear.
